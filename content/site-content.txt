About Us 
▪ Our Story: Hope For Families was created from a passion to support, uplift, 
and empower families and individuals from African and ethnic minority 
communities who often face social isolation, barriers to opportunities, cultural 
disconnection, and limited access to support systems. 
 
What began as a vision to create a safe and welcoming space for families has 
grown into a thriving community organisation delivering impactful programs 
that promote wellbeing, personal development, inclusion, creativity, and 
community cohesion. 
 
Through our initiatives, we provide opportunities for children, young people, 
women, and families to build confidence, develop new skills, improve 
wellbeing, create meaningful connections, and feel a true sense of belonging 
within the wider Scottish community. 
At Hope For Families, we believe every person deserves the opportunity to 
thrive regardless of background, ethnicity, or circumstance. 
 
▪ Mission Statement: Our mission is to empower young people, families, and 
individuals by tackling isolation, strengthening communities, and bridging the 
gap between African and ethnic minority communities and the wider Scottish 
community through inclusive support, opportunities, and meaningful 
community engagement. 
 
▪ Vision: To build a united, inclusive, and empowered society where African and 
ethnic minority communities thrive, barriers are broken, and every individual and 
family has the opportunity, support, and confidence to reach their full potential. 
 
▪ Values:  
• Inclusion – We create welcoming spaces where everyone feels valued, 
respected, and heard regardless of background or circumstance. 
• Empowerment – We inspire individuals, young people, and families to build 
confidence, develop their potential, and create positive futures. 
• Community – We believe strong communities are built through connection, 
collaboration, support, and unity. 
• Equality & Diversity – We celebrate cultural diversity and are committed to 
breaking barriers and promoting equal opportunities for all. 
• Compassion – We serve with kindness, understanding, and a genuine 
commitment to improving lives. 
• Integrity – We act with honesty, transparency, accountability, and 
professionalism in everything we do. 
• Growth & Opportunity – We are committed to creating meaningful 
opportunities that promote wellbeing, creativity, development, and lasting 
positive change.

Our Initiatives (Main Programs) 
Adolescent Development Initiative 
Overview + tagline: “Empowering teens with life skills for a brighter tomorrow. ” 
The Adolescent Development Initiative was created to support teenagers and young 
people through critical stages of personal growth, identity development, emotional 
wellbeing, and life preparation. 
Many young people from ethnic minority backgrounds face challenges such as low 
confidence, cultural barriers, social isolation, peer pressure, lack of positive role 
models, and limited access to opportunities. This initiative provides a safe, supportive, 
and inspiring environment where young people can learn valuable life skills, express 
themselves creatively, build confidence, and prepare for successful futures. 
The program combines practical training, mentorship, wellbeing support, creativity, and 
social engagement to help young people thrive emotionally, socially, and personally. 
Programs; 
▪ Hair Braiding Academy: The Hair Braiding Academy teaches young people 
practical hair braiding techniques while celebrating African culture, creativity, 
and identity. Participants learn professional braiding skills, hygiene practices, 
customer care, confidence building, and entrepreneurship basics. 
The program encourages creativity, patience, discipline, and cultural pride while 
also opening pathways to future employment or self-employment opportunities. 
 
▪ Under the Bonnet: A practical and empowering program introducing young 
people to basic vehicle maintenance, safety awareness, and essential life skills. 
 
▪ Makeup Class: Our Makeup Classes provide hands-on beauty training where 
participants learn makeup application techniques, skincare basics, hygiene 
practices, and creative beauty skills. 
The program promotes confidence, self-expression, creativity, and 
entrepreneurship while introducing participants to potential career pathways 
within the beauty industry. 
 
▪ Cooking and Pastry Baking: This practical life skills program teaches young 
people cooking, baking, nutrition awareness, kitchen safety, meal preparation, 
and teamwork. 
Participants gain independence, confidence, creativity, and healthy lifestyle 
habits while learning valuable everyday skills that support personal growth and 
wellbeing. 
 
▪ One on one Mentoring: Our mentoring program provides young people with 
guidance, encouragement, emotional support, and positive role models. 
Mentors help participants build confidence, set goals, overcome challenges, 
improve decision-making, and develop aspirations for education, employment, 
and personal success. 
 
 Community Mental Health and Wellbeing Initiative 
Overview + tagline: Supporting wellbeing, connection, and healthier communities. 
The Community Mental Health & Wellbeing Initiative focuses on reducing isolation, 
improving emotional wellbeing, encouraging social connection, and creating safe 
spaces where individuals feel supported and valued. 
Many individuals within African and ethnic minority communities experience loneliness, 
stress, anxiety, cultural barriers, and limited access to mental health support. This 
initiative promotes wellbeing through social activities, movement, conversations, peer 
support, and community engagement. 
The program encourages healthier lifestyles, emotional resilience, community 
belonging, and positive mental wellbeing. 
Programs; 
▪ Afro Dance Exercise: Afro Dance Exercise combines fitness, music, culture, and 
fun to improve physical and mental wellbeing. 
The sessions encourage participants to stay active, reduce stress, build 
confidence, improve fitness, and connect socially in an enjoyable and culturally 
inclusive environment. 
 
▪  Cozy Corner: Cozy Corner is a safe and welcoming community space where 
individuals can relax, connect, share experiences, receive peer support, and 
engage in meaningful conversations. 
The sessions help reduce isolation and promote emotional wellbeing, friendship, 
support networks, and community bonding. 
Little Stars Initiative (Ages 5–12) 
Overview + tagline: “Inspiring the leaders of tomorrow. ” 
Little Stars is designed to nurture, inspire, and support children aged 5–12 through 
educational, creative, recreational, and developmental activities. 
The initiative creates positive experiences where children can build confidence, 
discover talents, develop social skills, improve learning, and create lasting memories in 
a safe and inclusive environment. 
We aim to inspire children to dream big, believe in themselves, and develop the skills 
needed to succeed academically, socially, and personally. 
Programs; 
▪ STEM Club: Our STEM Club introduces children to science, technology, 
engineering, and mathematics through exciting hands-on activities, 
experiments, games, and creative learning. 
The program encourages curiosity, innovation, problem-solving, teamwork, and 
confidence in learning. 
 
▪ Fun Outings: Little Stars organises exciting educational and recreational outings 
including cinema trips, parks, museums, activity centres, and family 
experiences. 
These outings help children build friendships, confidence, social skills, cultural 
experiences, and positive childhood memories. 
 
FirstPass Football Academy 
Overview + tagline: “Together through Football. ” 
Weekly football training sessions focus on skill development, teamwork, fitness, 
confidence, leadership, discipline, and sportsmanship. 
Participants benefit physically, socially, and emotionally while building friendships and 
staying engaged in positive activities.

Food Bank & Café 
Overview + tagline: “Sharing meals, spreading hope. ” 
The Food Bank & Community Café supports families and individuals experiencing 
hardship, food insecurity, isolation, or financial difficulties. 
More than simply providing meals, the initiative creates a warm and welcoming 
environment where people can connect, access support, reduce isolation, and 
experience dignity and belonging. 
Through weekly meal support, refreshments, conversations, and community 
engagement, we aim to strengthen relationships, support wellbeing, and ensure nobody 
feels alone. 
▪ Meals once a week for families in need and isolated individuals. 
▪ Emphasis on social connections and community bonding.
